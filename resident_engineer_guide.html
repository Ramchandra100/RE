1. The Network Rack (The Cabinet)

You will see this glass cabinet in the server room. It contains two main parts.

A. PATCH PANEL (Passive/No Lights)
B. NETWORK SWITCH (Active/Blinking)

A. The Patch Panel

This is just a connector. Cables from the office walls end here. It has NO electricity and no lights.

B. The Network Switch

This is the brain. It has power and blinking lights.

  • Blinking Green: Good! Traffic is flowing.
  • Solid Orange: Warning (Speed mismatch or loop).
  • Off: Cable unplugged or dead port.

INTERNET Ethernet (RJ45)

The "Clip" is key. If this plastic tab on top is broken, the cable will fall out of the PC, causing random disconnects.

Tip: Always hear for the "Click" sound when plugging it in.

DISPLAY VGA vs HDMI

VGA (Old)

HDMI (New)

  • VGA: Has screws. Must be tightened or colors (pink/yellow) appear. No Audio.
  • HDMI: Flat connector. Carries Audio + Video. Push in firmly.

3. The Crimping Tool

You will use this to put new connectors on ethernet cables.

This tool does three things:

  1. Cuts the wire.
  2. Strips the blue outer plastic jacket.
  3. Crimps (presses) the RJ45 plastic head onto the wires.

Warning: Don't crimp too hard, or you crack the plastic clip. Don't crimp too soft, or the pins won't touch.
