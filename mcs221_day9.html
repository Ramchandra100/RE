🔄 1. OLTP vs. OLAP

Online Transaction Processing vs Online Analytical Processing — two sides of data management

📖 Explain

OLTP (Online Transaction Processing): Captures day-to-day transactions in real-time. Fast INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE, ACID compliant, highly normalized. Used for running business operations (e.g., ATM withdrawal, online order).

OLAP (Online Analytical Processing): Complex queries on historical data for business intelligence. Read-heavy, denormalized schemas (Star/Snowflake), supports multidimensional analysis. Used for strategic decisions (e.g., yearly sales trends).

🏧 Example:
• OLTP: You withdraw ₹500 from ATM — milliseconds, updates your balance.
• OLAP: Bank CEO analyzes "total withdrawals across all branches over 5 years" — minutes, helps decide where to open new ATMs.

🧊 2. Data Cube Operations: Roll-up & Drill-down

Navigating the concept hierarchy — aggregating up (Roll-up) vs drilling down into detail (Drill-down)

📖 Explain

OLAP views data as a multidimensional cube with dimensions like Time, Location, Product. Concept Hierarchy defines levels: Day → Month → Quarter → Year (Time).

  • 🔺 Roll-up (Aggregation): Moves UP the hierarchy — summarizes data (e.g., Monthly → Quarterly sales).
  • 🔻 Drill-down: Moves DOWN the hierarchy — splits data into finer detail (e.g., Country → State → City sales).
📊 Example:
• Roll-up: Viewing sales by Month → apply Roll-up → see sales by Quarter.
• Drill-down: Viewing sales by Country → Drill-down → see sales by individual States.

📝 Exam-Style Questions & Answers (June 2024 Pattern)

Q1. Differentiate between OLTP and OLAP. (High-weightage PYQ)
Answer: Purpose: OLTP runs daily business operations; OLAP supports strategic decision-making. Data: OLTP handles current transaction data; OLAP handles historical aggregated data. Queries: OLTP uses simple INSERT/UPDATE/DELETE; OLAP uses complex SELECT queries. Response: OLTP in milliseconds; OLAP in seconds/hours. Schema: OLTP uses normalized (3NF); OLAP uses denormalized (Star/Snowflake).
Q2. Discuss the OLAP data cube operation: Roll-up, with an example. (Direct PYQ)
Answer: Roll-up is an OLAP operation that performs aggregation by climbing up a concept hierarchy or reducing dimensions. It provides a summarized, broader view. Example: If sales data is at "City" level (Pune, Mumbai), a roll-up on Location dimension aggregates to "State" level (Maharashtra), summarizing city sales into one state total.
Q3. Discuss the OLAP data cube operation: Drill-down, with an example. (Direct PYQ)
Answer: Drill-down is the reverse of roll-up — it navigates from summarized data to detailed data by stepping down a concept hierarchy or adding dimensions. Example: Viewing total sales at "Yearly" level (2025 total), drill-down on Time dimension breaks it into "Quarter" or "Month" sales figures within that year.
Q4. Why must a data warehouse be kept separate from the operational (OLTP) database?
Answer: Separation is required because OLTP systems are optimized for high-concurrency, fast transactional writes with locking. OLAP queries are read-heavy, complex, and resource-intensive. Running OLAP queries on a live OLTP database would consume system resources, causing transaction delays or crashes, violating performance requirements for daily operations.
Q5. What is a "Concept Hierarchy" in dimensional modeling?
Answer: A concept hierarchy defines a sequence of mappings from low-level concepts to higher-level, more general concepts within a dimension. It enables OLAP operations like roll-up and drill-down. For example, in Time dimension: Day → Month → Quarter → Year. In Location: City → District → State → Country.
